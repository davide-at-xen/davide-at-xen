Candidate Privacy Notice

Xen Quantum Ltd

Last updated: 13 February 2026

Xen Quantum Ltd (“Xen Quantum”, “we”, “our”, or “us”) is committed to protecting your personal data. This Candidate Privacy Notice explains how we collect, use, and store personal information provided as part of our recruitment process.

1. Who We Are

Xen Quantum Ltd is the data controller responsible for your personal data.

If you have any questions about this notice or your personal data, please contact: careers@xenquantum.com

2. What Information We Collect

When you apply for a role with us, we may collect:

  • Your name and contact details (email, phone number)
  • CV and employment history
  • Education and qualifications
  • Cover letter or supporting statements
  • Links to professional profiles (e.g. LinkedIn, GitHub)
  • Any additional information you voluntarily provide

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data unless you choose to provide it.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal data solely for recruitment purposes, including:

  • Assessing your suitability for a role
  • Communicating with you about your application
  • Managing our hiring process
  • Keeping records of recruitment decisions

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

We process your personal data under:

  • Legitimate interests — to assess candidates and manage recruitment.
  • Consent — where you agree to allow us to retain your application for future roles.

5. Data Storage and Sharing

We collect applications via Microsoft Forms (Microsoft 365). Your data may be stored securely using:

  • Microsoft 365
  • Internal business systems used for recruitment

We do not sell or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes.

Your data may be processed outside the UK or EU by our service providers, subject to appropriate safeguards in line with UK GDPR requirements.

6. How Long We Keep Your Data

If your application is unsuccessful, we will retain your data for up to 6 months from the date of submission, unless you request deletion sooner.

If you are successful, your data will become part of your employee record.

7. Your Rights

Under UK data protection law, you have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal data
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Object to or restrict processing
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO)

You can exercise your rights by contacting us at careers@xenquantum.com.

8. Updates to This Notice

We may update this notice from time to time. The latest version will always be available on our website.
